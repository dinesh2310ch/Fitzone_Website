Calories Checker
How can I estimate daily calorie intake considering my weight, height, age, and activity level, since BMI alone doesn't provide that information?

This calorie intake calculator estimates daily calorie needs based on your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR), which considers factors like age, gender, weight, and height. It then adjusts for your activity level and weight management goals (maintain, lose, gain weight) by creating calorie deficits or surpluses. Remember, this is a general estimate, and consulting a healthcare professional is recommended for personalized guidance, especially for significant weight changes.

BMI Categories:

Underweight (BMI < 18.5)
Normal Weight (BMI = 18.5 - 24.9)
Overweight (BMI = 25 - 29.9)
Obese (BMI >= 30)

For Moderate Weight Loss (1-2 pounds per week):

Underweight: 10-25% calorie deficit
Normal Weight: 15-25% calorie deficit
Overweight: 20-30% calorie deficit
Obese: 25-35% calorie deficit
